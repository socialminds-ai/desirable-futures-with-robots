# Desirable Futures with Robots Workshops Series

## Context:

- Extremely rapid changes brought by GenAI
- within 1-2y, likely to have fully autonomous robots in our daily life, both at home and at work
- High potential of disruption

## Problem statement:

- The narrative around AI agents and autonomous robots is build around the idea of ‘improving human efficiency/productivity’
- (‘robots for dull/dangerous/dirty work’ is a variant of it)
- This narrative contains intrinsically the idea of a competition with humans


- This is already visible with AI agents, only 3y after the introduction of ChatGPT
- Likely going to be worse with robots, as they might be competing with every aspects of our life.


## Position statement:

- Another narrative is possible and desirable
- Not about ‘AI to increase humans’, but ‘AI as an alternative form of cognition, alongside human’

**Can we invent/define: a caring, feminist, ‘European’ narrative for social robots?**


## Workshop series:

- distributed format, across the world
- diverse target groups (children, adolescents, older adults, warehouse workers, etc.), across different cultures and different socio-economic contexts
- one final event, april 2027, where we will present the results of the workshops and discuss them with a large audience (including policy makers, industry, media, etc.)

- workshops led by local facilitators (primarily, members of the Human-Robot Interaction research community), the exact protocol might vary (depending on target group), but shared reporting format to be able to compare and aggregate results across workshops
- scale: ~100 groups in total, but staged 10 by 10, so that we can iteratively refine the approach (at the beginning at least)
- main scafolding question: ‘what if’ questions, taking the opposite stance to the dominant narrative about robots; must be answered from a positive/desirable perspective for the human society

## Objectives at the end:

- view of the desirable future with robots
- focus on desirable futures: build utopia, not dystopia
- robots have to be part of the picture
- but: think beyond techno-solutionism
  - Raquel
    - process can/should be: (1) what future do I want? (2) how robot fit into that picture? how can they help to achieve that?
      - ‘the worst thing for me would be… XXX; how could robots help me avoid it? what would such a robot look like?’
    - rather than: (1) I have a robot; (2) what can I do with it?
  - however: need to ensure we also think about possible future that would only be possible with robot
- could be the robot role, but also the robot shape, embodiment, physical place
- the workshops can not replace in-situ long term deployments of robots to imagine future roles (due to mutual shaping + natural adaptation in the ecosystem), but the basic purpose of the workshops is more about planting seeds for alternative narrative building
- Big picture:
  - purpose is not exactly to generate concrete, operational ideas for robots in the future (even though, it is also a nice side effect)
  - instead: challenge the view of ‘robots as obedient servitor with superhuman powers’ and build alternative narrative around the role and place of robots
  - plant the seeds
    - for people to feel safe today, in the face of robots arriving
    - for designing desirable futures (10 y horizon) with robots
